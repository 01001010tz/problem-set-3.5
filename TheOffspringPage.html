The Offspring


My Thoughts

Though I've been listening to them for a long time now, I never actually got around to listening to almost only Offspring for an extended period of time. Furthermore, I only really focused on the two or three albums I knew for the longest time (being Ixnay on the Hombre and Smash). After I branched out into Americana and eventually Ignition, I found myself liking them more than I ever had. Some titles, as with Green Day, did eventually span more into "emo" music as a genre and strayed from the original sound of early Offspring, and I definitely disliked those songs and albums more. In their first few albums, I enjoyed the faster music and almost Nirvana or Green Day -esque guitar riffs as well as Dexter Holland's vocals, which like Billie Joe Armstrong's, has always had a unique quality I've never quite been able to put into words but nevertheless love. All of the pieces of the band come together really well to create a classic, well-oiled punk rock machine. I will admit, though, no matter how good their music was I never liked some of their lyrics, especially on Americana, which was sort of the beginning of the end of my favorite era of Offspring music. Such lyrics as "Have you ever walked through a room / But it was more like the room passed through you" (Have You Ever) and "Feelings" repeated more than 2 dozen times throughout a three-minute song (Feelings) felt kind of lazy and even pretentious in how they were clearly trying to sound introspective and pander to an audience looking more for "emo Offspring" than "original Offspring". However, all in all they've been one of my favorite bands that I was actually lucky enough to see live a few years ago.
