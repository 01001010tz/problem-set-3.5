Primus


My Thoughts

I didn't appreciate Primus until much later, and when I was younger it only stood out to me because I thought the strange songs such as "Pudding Time" had funny titles or lyrics. The first song I really listened to because I liked the sound of it and not the title name was Jerry Was a Racecar Driver, though after I continued listening to other songs on the same album I quickly realized how much I was missing out on. Unfortunately, I came to later neglect it until we actually went to see them live, which completely redefined Primus for me. I had always figured the impossibly fast and distorted bass riffs were just the work of the lead guitar or some post-production effects that couldn't really be used to play music practically. But when I went to see Primus live, I was blown away by how incredibly talented (and admittedly weird) Les Claypool was. His fantastic bass abilities are almost objectively unrivaled, and more or less completely redefined the way I listened to music. Before then, I focused mostly on the lead guitar riffs and drum (as both my dad and I drum, making it a lot more focal), seeing the bass mostly as a way to either supplement the lead riff or keep time as a more simple drum part would. However, after listening to Primus and realizing the true potential bass guitars held musically, I listened for them a lot more prominently in all of the music I listened to from then on. Furthermore, Primus further distorts the way I had thought of music in that instead of the guitar taking most of the lead, bass supplementing to it, and drums playing off of either of the other two as standard with the music styles I was accustomed to, Les and Herb (Primus' drummer) dominate most of the music, while the electric guitar seems to only exist for one or two one-off notes played at insignificant parts of the song. And yet it works-- Les and Herb play so well off of each other and so well even individually the unorthodox dynamic works, and sounds amazing. Primus' strange but spectacular style was so good I've seen them live more than once, and plan on seeing them again whenever possible. If you're ever given the chance, I highly recommend it.
